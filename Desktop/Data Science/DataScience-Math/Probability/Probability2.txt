Probability Distribution 
========================
A probability distribution is a mathematical description of how probabilities are assigned to the possible outcomes of a random variable.

In simple terms, it tells us how likely each outcome is.

Example 1: Tossing a Fair Coin

Possible outcomes:
Heads (H)
Tails (T)

Probability distribution:

Outcome	Probability
Heads	0.5
Tails	0.5

The probabilities add up to 1:
0.5 + 0.5 = 1


Example 2: Rolling a Fair Die

Possible outcomes: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6

Probability distribution:

Outcome	Probability
1	1/6
2	1/6
3	1/6
4	1/6
5	1/6
6	1/6

Again, the probabilities sum to 1.




Types of Probability Distributions
----------------------------------
1)Discrete Probability Distribution
	> Used when outcomes are countable.
	> Examples: coin tosses, dice rolls.
	> Common distributions: Binomial, Poisson.

2) Continuous Probability Distribution
	> Used when outcomes can take any value within a range.
	> Examples: height, weight, temperature.
	> Common distributions: Normal distribution, Exponential distribution.




Binomial Distribution
======================
The binomial distribution is the discrete probability distribution that gives
only two possible results in an experiment, either success or failure.

ex:	
	If we toss a coin, there could be only two possible outcomes head or tail.

A Single success/failure test is also called a Bernoulli trial or Bernoulli experiment
and a series of outcomes is called Benoulli process.
