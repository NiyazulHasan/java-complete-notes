Exponential Distribution 
=========================
The exponential distribution is a way to model how long you have to wait until something happens.
It is used when events happen randomly but at a constant average rate.

ex:

Imagine:

Bus arrives randomly but on average every 10 minutes
You are waiting at the bus stop

The exponential distribution tells:
“What is the probability you will wait x minutes?”


Poisson vs Exponential

> Counts how many events happen (How many calls arrive in 10 minutes)

> Measures time between events (Time until next phone call)

Question 
=========
A call center receives calls at an average rate of 2 calls per minute.
What is the probability that the next call comes after 1 minute?

> Identify the distribution

Rate: λ=2 calls per minute
We want: P(X>1)

> Use formula for waiting time

	 -λx
 P(X>x)=e


> Substitute values

	        -2⋅1
	P(X>1)=e

		-2
	P(X>1)=e
	
> Final answer

Diagram: Probability5.2

    P(X>1)≈0.135

There is about a 13.5% chance that you will have to wait more than 1 minute for the next call.
