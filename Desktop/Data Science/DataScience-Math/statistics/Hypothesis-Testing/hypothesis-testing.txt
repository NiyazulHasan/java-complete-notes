Hypothesis Testing 
==================
In data science, a hypothesis is a testable assumption about data, patterns, or relationships that you try to confirm or reject using analysis.

Imagine a company changes the color of a “Buy Now” button from blue to green.

After one week:

	Old button conversion rate = 5%
	New button conversion rate = 6%

Question:

	Did the green button actually improve conversions?
	OR
	Could the 1% increase have happened just by random chance?

	Hypothesis testing helps answer this scientifically.


We have two types of hypothesis.

1. Null Hypothesis (H₀)

2. Alternative Hypothesis (H₁ or Ha)

1. Null Hypothesis (H₀)
-------------------------
This is the default assumption.

> It says there is no effect, no difference, or no relationship

Example:
	H₀: The new button does NOT change conversion rate
	H₀: Mean sales before = Mean sales after


2. Alternative Hypothesis (H₁ or Hₐ)
--------------------------------------
This is what you want to prove or support.

> It says there IS an effect, difference, or relationship

Example:
	H₁: The new button improves conversion rate
	H₁: Mean sales after ≠ Mean sales before

================================
We have five steps to perform hypothesis testing.

step1:
-----
	Setup a hypothesis.

		Here we will decide what Hₐ (Alternate Hypothesis) we need to take.

step2:
-----
	Setpup a significance level.
	ex:
		Here we will set significance level.
		ex:
			0.05%, 1%, 5% and 10% 

		Note:
		-----
			Hypothesis testing failed if we take significance level above 10%.

step3:
------
	Setting a test Critarian.
	ex:
		Test critarain completely depend on sample size.
		ex:
			t-test  <30
			z-test  >= 30
			f-test 	Comparing two variances  
			ANOVA   Used to compare 3 or more groups

step4:
------
	Doing computation.
	ex:
		Add the values in a test formulea to perform calculations.
	

step5:
------
	Making Decisions.

		Here Null Hypothesis can be true or false.

				---------			--------
		> H₀ is true	Correct Decision		Type-I Error (α - alpha)
