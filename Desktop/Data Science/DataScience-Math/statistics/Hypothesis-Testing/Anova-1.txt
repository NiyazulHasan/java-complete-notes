Anova (Analysis of Variance)
============================
ANOVA (Analysis of Variance) is a statistical hypothesis-testing method used to determine whether the means of three or more groups are significantly different from each other.

Instead of comparing groups one pair at a time, ANOVA tests all groups simultaneously by analyzing the variation within groups and between groups.

Need of Anova
--------------
Suppose you want to compare the average exam scores of students taught using three different teaching methods. Conducting multiple t-tests increases the chance of making a false conclusion. ANOVA provides a single test for comparing all groups.

Assuptions of Anova
===================
> Each population having Normal Distribution.

> The population from which the samples are drawn have the equal variance.
i.e s1^2 = s2^2 = s3^2 ........ = sn^2

> Each sample is drawn randomly & they are independent.

Types of ANOVA
--------------
> One-Way ANOVA
	One factor (e.g., fertilizer type).

> Two-Way ANOVA
	Two factors (e.g., fertilizer type and sunlight level).

>Repeated Measures ANOVA
	Same subjects measured multiple times.
