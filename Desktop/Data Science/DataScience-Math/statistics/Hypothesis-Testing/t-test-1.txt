t-test
======
A t-test is a statistical test used in parametric hypothesis testing to determine whether the difference between means is likely real or just due to random chance.

Ex:

Imagine you want to know whether two groups are different:

	Do students taught with Method A score differently from students taught with Method B?
	Do men and women have different average heights?

> t distribution has a bell shaped curve and symmetry similar to normal distribution.
> The shape of the "t-distribution" varies with the change in degree of freedom.
> It is less peaked than normal distribution at center and more peaked in the tails.


Diagram: t-test-1.1

We have two types of t-test.

1) Paired (Dependent)

2) Unpaired (Independent)

1) Paired (Dependent)
---------------------
A paired t-test is used when you measure the same people twice.

Ex:

A doctor wants to know whether a tablet reduces blood pressure.

He selects 10 patients and measures their blood pressure:
> Before taking the tablet
> After taking the tablet for 30 days


2) Unpaired (Independent)
--------------------------
An unpaired t-test is used when you compare two different groups of people.

Ex:

A doctor wants to compare two tablets (Paracetamol).

Tablet A
Tablet B

He randomly assigns:

> 10 patients to Tablet A
> 10 different patients to Tablet B

After 30 days, blood pressure is measured.
