2) Paired t test (Testing the significance of an observed Correlation Coefficient)
==================================================================================
Diagram: t-test-4.1

Question1
----------
A random sample of 27 pairs of observations from a normal population gives a correlation coefficient of 0.55 is likely that the variables in the population are correlated?

Let consider:
		Ho : Correlation = Not significant
		Ha : Correlation ≠ Not significant

Given Data:
		n = 27
		r = 0.55
		
		        0.55		--------
		t = --------------  =  | 27 -2 
		    --------------    V
		   V 1 - (0.55)^2


		        0.55		-----
		t = --------------  x  | 25
		    --------------    V
		   V 1 - 0.3025


		        0.55		
		t = --------------  x 5  
		      -------- 
		     V 0.6975		

                     0.55 * 5        2.75
		t = -----------  =  ------   = 3.293 (tcal)
                      0.835          0.835
	
	ν (nu) degree of freedom 
ex:
	ν (nu)  = n - 2
		= 27 - 2 
		= 25

Diagram:  t-test-2.2 (With the help of diagram we can find the result)

	t	= 2.060     
	0.05,25

	tcal > ttab  

	So Ho cannot be accepted.



Question2
----------
Is a correlation coefficient of 0.6 significant? if obtained from a random sample of 11 pairs of values from a normal population?

Let consider:
		Ho : Correlation = Not significant
		Ha : Correlation ≠ Not significant

Given Data:
		n = 11
		r = 0.6
		
		        0.6		--------
		t = --------------  =  | 11 - 2 
		    --------------    V
		   V 1 - (0.6)^2


		        0.6		-----
		t = --------------  x  | 9
		    --------------    V
		   V 1 - 0.36


		        0.6		
		t = --------------  x 3 
		      -------- 
		     V 0.64		

                       0.6            
		t = -----------x 3 	
			0.8

			1.8
		t =  ------   = 2.25 (tcal)
                        0.8         
	
	ν (nu) degree of freedom 
ex:
	ν (nu)  = n - 2
		= 11 - 2 
		= 9

Diagram:  t-test-2.2 (With the help of diagram we can find the result)

	t	= 2.262     
	0.05,9

	tcal < ttab  

	So Ho is accepted.
