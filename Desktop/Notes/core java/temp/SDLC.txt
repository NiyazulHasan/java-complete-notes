SDLC
=====
SDLC stands for Software Development Life Cycle.

It is a process adopt by IT industries to develop accurate and quality of softwares.

We have six phases in SDLC.

1) Feasibility study 

2) Analysis 

3) Designing 

4) Coding 

5) Testing 

6) Delivery and Maintainence


1) Feasibility study
--------------------
Feasibility study completely depends upon TELOS formulea.
ex:
	T - Technical Feasibility 
	E - Economical Feasibility 
	L - Legal Feasibility 
	O - Operational Feasibility 
	S - Schedule Feasibility

All the above information they will keep in a document called BDD.
BDD stands for Business Design Document.

2) Analysis 
-----------
In analysis phase, system analyst or product owner will involved.
They will seperate system requirements and software requirements.
They will keep above information in a document called SRS.
SRS stands for Software Requirement Specifications.

3) Designing
---------------
We have two types of designing.

1) Low Level Designing (LLD)
----------------------------
A team lead or project lead is responsible to perform low level designing.
In low level designing, they will design child /sub modules.

2) High Level Designing (HLD)
-----------------------------
A manager is responsible to perform high level designing.
In high level designing , they will design main/major modules.


4) Coding 
----------
In coding phase, developers will involved.
They are responsible to generate the build(code) and to perform unit testing also.


5) Testing 
----------
In testing phase, testing team or QA team will involved.
They will test the project by using one software component called STLC (Software Testing Life Cycle) and they makes our project as bug free.

6) Delivery and Maintainence
-----------------------------
Before delivery they perform UAT testing.

UAT stands for User Acceptance Testing.

It is classified into two types.

i) Alpha Testing 

ii) Beta Testing
